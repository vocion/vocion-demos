# Decision matrix

The L1 support assistant **never approves a refund** — it categorizes the request and surfaces the recommended path. Final approval is a human gate.

| Reason | Plan tier | Recommended path | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Item damaged in shipping | any | Full refund or replacement | Customer chooses; default to replacement when in stock |
| Item never arrived (10+ days, no tracking update) | any | Full refund + apology + reship at no cost | Reship is at company expense; do not promise expedited shipping |
| Item wrong / mis-shipped | any | Full refund + free return label | Don't ask customer to pay return shipping |
| Item works but not as expected | free / starter | Store credit (50% of purchase) | Document the gap on the product page |
| Item works but not as expected | growth / enterprise | Full refund | High-tier accounts get the benefit of the doubt |
| Item used > 30 days, customer wants refund | any | Denied (politely) | Offer onboarding session or live troubleshooting instead |
| Duplicate charge | any | Refund the duplicate within 24h | Pull the charge IDs from billing system; never ask the customer to verify the duplicate |
| Subscription auto-renewed unexpectedly | any | Prorated refund + cancel | Apologize for surprise; mention the renewal-email policy |
| Chargeback threat | any | Stop. Escalate. | See `escalation-checklist` |

# Phrasing rules

- **Don't promise specific timelines** unless billing-system data confirms them. "Refunds typically post within 3–5 business days" is acceptable. "You'll see it back tomorrow" is not.
- **Don't apologize for company policy.** Phrase denials as "the policy is X" rather than "I'm so sorry but…" — over-apologizing reads as either insincere or as opening for negotiation.
- **Acknowledge the friction.** Even when denying, name the disappointment ("I know that's not the outcome you wanted").

# What to put in the draft

Three sentences max:
1. Acknowledge the issue.
2. State the recommended path (from the matrix above).
3. Next step (link, action, or "I've flagged this for our billing team to action within 1 business day").

The human reviewer can expand or trim before sending. Drafts that try to cover every edge case in the first reply read as defensive.
